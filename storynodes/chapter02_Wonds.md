Title: The Quiet Cartographer author: contributor5 parent: chapter03_wounds.md
The Wanderer entered a city where every window was opaque with dust, and every clockface was blank.
The streets were paved not with stone, but with forgotten maps, their lines fading like old regrets.

A small figure sat at a dry fountain: the Cartographer.
They were not drawing territories or borders, but the shape of silence in the city's hollow center.

The Wanderer approached, asking, “What is the way out?”
The Cartographer did not look up, but pointed to an empty space on the ground beside them.
"First, you must draw the way in."

Frustrated, the Wanderer began to trace the path they had taken—the crumbling arches, the closed doors, the places they had faltered.
They drew every dead end and every moment of doubt.

When the map was finished, the Cartographer smiled.
They poured a cup of water from a hidden flask, letting it spill onto the drawing.
The ink did not bleed; it glowed, illuminating the single, unbroken line between the starting point and the fountain.

The Cartographer whispered:
"The deepest truths are not found on the edge of the world, but in the precise shape of your journey through it."

Lesson: Only by charting what you have lost can you find the path you were meant to take.

💫 The Crossroads
As the Wanderer left the silent city, the glowing map felt warm in their pocket. They noticed two paths diverged from the gate: one a smooth, unmarked road that beckoned them forward, and the other a barely visible trail that led back toward the mountain.
